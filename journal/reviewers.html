Reviewer Guidelines


Peer Review Model for ASFI Research Journal


Objective:

The primary objective of the peer review process at ASFI Research Journal is to ensure the quality, integrity, and relevance of scholarly articles submitted for publication. Peer review plays a pivotal role in maintaining the standards of excellence and contributing to the advancement of knowledge within the African context.


Peer Review Process:

Submission:

Authors submit their manuscripts electronically through the journal's online submission system. Manuscripts should adhere to the journal's formatting guidelines and clearly present original research within the scope of ASFI Research Journal.


Editorial Review:

The Editorial Board conducts an initial assessment to ensure adherence to the journal's scope and guidelines. Manuscripts meeting initial criteria proceed to the peer review process.


Reviewer Assignment:

The Editor-in-Chief assigns manuscripts to qualified reviewers based on their expertise and the subject matter of the submission. Reviewers are selected for their knowledge in the specific field and commitment to providing constructive feedback.


Revision of Manuscripts

Revised manuscripts must be submitted via ASFIRJ submission portal. Authors must ensure that the revised manuscript adheres to ASFIRJ formats and requirements. The revised manuscript must include the following items:

(1) Responses to Reviewers’ Comments: This must include point-by-point responses to the comments and concerns raised by the reviewers, editor, and editorial office. The authors’ responses should clearly specify how they have addressed the concerns raised, including clarity on any changes made on the manuscript, tables, figures, and other parts of the manuscript.

(2) Marked Revised Manuscript: The revised main text of the manuscript should be done using the Microsoft Word’s Track Changes feature, which should show every alteration made to the manuscript. If the authors are unable to use the Microsoft Word's Track Changes feature, they should underline the texts that have been added to the manuscript and then strikethrough the texts that have been deleted. Changes made to the tables and figures should also be indicated. Authors should use a continuous line numbering throughout the Marked Manuscript.


Review Evaluation:

Reviewers provide detailed and constructive feedback on the manuscript's strengths and weaknesses. Feedback includes recommendations for improvement, clarification, and identification of any ethical concerns.


Decision Making:

The Editor-in-Chief reviews the feedback and makes an editorial decision based on the reviewers' recommendations. Decisions include acceptance, revision, or rejection. Authors receive detailed feedback to guide revisions.


Author Revisions:

Authors are encouraged to revise and resubmit their manuscripts based on the reviewers' comments and editorial feedback. Revised manuscripts undergo further evaluation to ensure that recommendations have been addressed appropriately.


Final Decision:

The final decision is made based on the quality of the revised manuscript. Accepted manuscripts proceed to the production process, preparing for publication.


Reviewer Expectations:

Reviewers are expected to provide timely, unbiased, and constructive feedback. Confidentiality must be maintained throughout the review process. Reviewers should disclose any conflicts of interest that may compromise their objectivity. Constructive criticism and guidance are essential to fostering the growth of authors and maintaining the journal's high standards.
ASFI Research Journal values the commitment of reviewers in upholding the integrity of scholarly research within the African context. The peer review process contributes to the journal's mission of promoting excellence and advancing knowledge across diverse disciplines.

At ASFIRJ, we prioritize our publishers' expectations. With clear guidance, we ensure effective management and delivery. Join us in upholding excellence in research publishing.
